\maketitle
\noindent This document bundles every figure and table of the analysis. Supervised baselines are evaluated on all cases via 5-fold subject-level out-of-fold cross-validation; TiRex-2 is zero-shot (inherently held-out). Cross-dataset transfer (Fig.~6, Table~7) uses the external MOVER cohort. Inference is case-clustered bootstrap throughout.

(a) Early warning, two views at a fixed operating point (spec.\ $\geq$0.90): (left) detection yield --- of all impending hypotension events, the fraction flagged at least $t$ min ahead (the $t{=}0$ intercept is sensitivity, the decay is the lead-time distribution); (right) the alarm-burden trade-off --- sensitivity vs false alarms per hour as the alarm threshold sweeps (the clinical alert operating characteristic). (b) Operating characteristics at spec.\ $\geq$0.90: a 1$\times$4 strip, one panel per metric (sensitivity, PPV, NPV, F1), the four models overlaid across horizons. (d) Subgroup robustness: per-subgroup hypotension AUROC at 5 min --- TiRex-2 (point + case-clustered 95\% CI) with TFT, PatchTST and Chronos-Bolt overlaid; TiRex-2 stays close to its overall value across every subgroup.
